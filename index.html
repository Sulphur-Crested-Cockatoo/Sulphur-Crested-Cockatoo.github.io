Sulphur Crested Cockatoos

Jump to sections: Distribution,
Behavior

The Sulphur Crested Cockatoo is a relatively large bird ranging from 17.5 to 21.5in (45 to 55 cm) from the family Cacatuidae (meaning 'cockatoos'). They are highly intelligent birds and have been known to repeat sounds, talk, and even dance. Sulphur crested cockatoos live in northern and eastern Australia.

Distribution

In Australia, sulphur-crested cockatoos can be found mostly in the north and east, ranging from the Kimberley to Tasmania. There are four main subspecies:

  1. C. g. triton, commonly called the Triton cockatoo is Found in New Guinea and the surrounding islands
  2. C. g. eleonora also known as the Eleonora cockatoo Restricted to the Aru Islands in the Maluku province of eastern Indonesia
  3. C. g. fitzroyi commonly called the Mathews cockatoo found in Northern Australia from West Australia to the Gulf of Carpentaria
  4. C. g. galerita, aslo known as the Greater sulphur-crested cockatoo. They are found from Cape York to Tasmania.

Behavior

Sulphur crested cockatoos are naturally curious and highly intelligent. In Sydney, Australia, they have learned how to open garbage bins as a source of food. In captivity, sulphur crested cockatoos have been known to dance to music. One sulphur crested cockatoo, named Snowball, has an entire 14 different dance moves! They can live from 20 to 40 years in the wild and up to 70 years in captivity. Sulphur crested cockatoos also eat clay in the wild to detoxify their food, a process called geophagy. They produce a powder to waterproof their wings, unlike most birds who produce oil as a waterproofing.
